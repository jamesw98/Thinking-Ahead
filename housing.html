Pros and Cons of On-Campus and Off-Campus Housing

On-Campus

Off-Campus

Pros

Pros
  • Close to classes
  • On-campus students have are within 15 minutes walking distance from almost every academic building
  • This means you will be less likely to be late to class
  • This also means you are closer to professors if you need help during their office hours
  • Close to dining halls
  • On-campus students are significantly close to dining halls compaired to off-campus students
  • It is easy to grab a quick meal from one of the many great dining halls
  • You won't have to cook at all
  • Easier to meet new people
  • You are going to be living in close proximity to a bunch of people, so there will be many opportunities to meet new people
  • There are often hall events at least once a month, depending on your RA
  • Traditional college experience
  • By living in a dorm, you are going to get the "traditional college experience", which could be a pro or a con, depending on your point of view
  • Until this year (2019-2020), you were required to live in a dorm your first year
  • More freedom
  • Living off-campus gives you significantly more freedom to do what you want to do
  • There is no RA telling you can and can't do
  • This doesn't mean you can go crazy though, you want to keep your security deposit
  • Privacy
  • Depending on where you decide to live, you will have a room and most likely a bathroom to yourself
  • Compared to dorms, this is a huge upgrade, no more sharing a bathroom with the rest of the hall
  • More space
  • With an apartment, you are going to have signicantly more room to store your personal belongings
  • You won't feel cramped like most people feel in dorms
  • Some apartments even allow pets!
  • Less expensive (sometimes)
  • Depending on which apartment you choose and how many roommates you have, off-campus living can be cheaper than living on campus
  • Rent it usually lower
  • You don't have to pay as many on-campus fees
  • You are (probably) going to spend less money on dining halls and spend more on cooking for yourself

Cons

Cons
  • Less freedom
  • All dorms have RA's, this means you won't be able to do certain things that you might want to do
  • Most on-campus students don't have cars, so you will be limited to where the busses can take you for going places in your freetime
  • Small rooms and sharing space
  • Most of the dorm rooms at Tech are pretty small and you will have a roommate
  • If you have never shared a room with someone before this can be a big adjustment
  • You will also have to share a bathroom with at least one other person, and most likey share it with a lot of people
  • Annoying neighbors
  • There isn't much you can do if your neighbors are being loud or doing something annoying
  • You can report them to the RA, but there isn't too much they can do
  • You aren't guarenteed to have annoying neighbors, but there is more than likely going to be at least one
  • Farther from campus
  • Living off-campus obviously means you are off campus
  • You either have to walk, ride the bus, or drive to campus now
  • This also means you are probably going to rely less on dining halls for your meals
  • Less reliable services
  • Depending on where you decide to live, the internet is going to be slower than the dorm internet
  • You are also going to have to pay separately for things like cable and internet
  • Also depening on where you decide to live, getting things repaired is going to be slower than the dorms
  • More expensive (sometimes)
  • Again, depening on where you decide to live, it could be more expensive than living in a dorm, especially if you decide to live on your own
  • If you don't cook, you are still going to pay a lot for food
